## Asking questions

Fundamentally, statistics is about answering big questions with little information. For instance, we may be interested **to know** the average height of male humans that live in some large city. We might be interested determine if a drug **has an effect** to treat a specific disease. We might be interested to **model how** murders grow along with more gun sales. We might be interested in **predicting** tomorrow's market changes.

The question asked is of fundamental importance. From the question, we determine the best course of action to take to answer it. The question has an impact on the collection of the data, on the statistical method used to analyze the data, and on the validity of our conclusions.
