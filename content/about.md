Hello, my name is Luca and this is my blog, where I occasionally post about the things that
interest me.


I've had a keen interest in things that are alive, and have studied biology all my life.
I have a Master's degree in Cellular and Molecular Biology, and have combined my interest in biology with my passion for computer science late in my Master Degree course, so now I work as a Bioinformatician.
I am based in Turin, Italy.

I'm currently working as a PHD candidate at Prof. Munaron Lab, under the supervision of Dr. Ruffinatti at the University of Turin.
The goal of my PhD is to profile the gene expression in tumor cells to detect deregulations at a whole-transportome level.
You can find most of my work in my Github page ([here](https://github.com/MrHedmad/)) or my lab's github page ([TCP-Lab, here](https://github.com/TCP-Lab/)).
I mostly code in `R` and `Python`, although I've recently taken a liking for the `Rust` programming language and have started creating command line tools with it.

During my PhD and in general research work I've come in contact with the ideas of Open, Transparent and Reproducible Science, and now I'm interested in developing my skills in data management, experimental design, reproducibility, scientific philosophy and transparent, society-driven science.
I'm currently chairman of the [Open Science Community Torino](https://osc-international.com/osc-torino/), and I have taken the first ever italian data stewardship course in Turin, Italy, during most of 2024.

In my spare time I'm a PC gamer, and I play a ton of board games and Dungeons and Dragons.

## My tools

Some of my favourite day-to-day tools include:
- [Arch Linux](https://archlinux.org/)

  Just to boast that I use Arch. There is a post somewhere here that details
  what I use on my personal laptop.
  Arch is just leagues better than other flavours of Linux thanks to `pacman`
  and the official and unofficial repositories. Being a rolling release is also
  a must for me. I think my old Ubuntu systems broke at every release update.

- [NeoVim](https://neovim.io/)

  I've been using NeoVim for a long time now, and I think I won't ever go back.
  It excels at both speed and convenience.

- [The Corne keyboard](https://github.com/foostan/crkbd)

  After I bought this keyboard I cannot go back to anything else. It is
  expensive, looks amazing, a hell to put together, can be configured to your
  liking and grabs everyone's attention when they walk in the lab.
  The keys are [Cherry Whites](https://deskthority.net/wiki/Cherry_MX_White)
  with blank white keycaps (to force me to [touch type](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Touch_typing)).
  It is programmed with [`via`](https://caniusevia.com/), so it sends the
  canonical `en-US` keycodes but with the [Hands Down Reference](https://sites.google.com/alanreiser.com/handsdown/home)
  layout.

  I like to think of myself as a keyboard goblin.

- [An Ipad Pro](https://www.apple.com/ipad-pro/)

  I got an ipad as a gift from me (and my SO) for the start of my PhD course at the university of Turin.

  I have access to an intel NUC at the lab, and my PC at home, so the ipad is what gets carried back and forth from home to the lab, daily. Its capabilities to markup text and take notes are unsurpassed, and is a hub for all of my documents and things to read.
