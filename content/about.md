## My tools

Some of my favourite day-to-day tools include:
- [Arch Linux](https://archlinux.org/)

  Just to boast that I use Arch. There is a post somewhere here that details
  what I use on my personal laptopa.
  Arch is just leagues better than other flavours of Linux thanks to `pacman`
  and the official and unofficial repositories. Being a rolling release is also
  a must for me. I think my old Ubuntu systems broke at every release update.

- [NeoVim](https://neovim.io/)

  As my source code editor, I choose NeoVim with [VsCode](https://code.visualstudio.com/)
  as the backup alternative. The ability to use `vim` shortcuts (even though I
  only know of a handful of them) is priceless to me. As a plus, I also get to
  use my editor without leaving the terminal.

- [RStudio](https://www.rstudio.com/)

  To edit R source there is little better than RStudio. RStudio server can also
  be installed on a remote machine to let you work remotely from the desktop. It
  is not the best IDE, but the best when it comes to R.

- [The Corne keyboard](https://github.com/foostan/crkbd)

  After I bought this keyboard I cannot go back to anything else. It is
  expensive, looks amazing, a hell to put together, can be configured to your
  liking and grabs everyone's attention when they walk in the lab.
  The keys are [Cherry Whites](https://deskthority.net/wiki/Cherry_MX_White)
  with blank white keycaps (to force me to [touch type](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Touch_typing)).
  It is programmed with [`via`](https://caniusevia.com/), so it sends the
  canonical `en-US` keycodes but with the [Hands Down Reference](https://sites.google.com/alanreiser.com/handsdown/home)
  layout.

  I like to think of myself as a keyboard goblin.

on the internet.
